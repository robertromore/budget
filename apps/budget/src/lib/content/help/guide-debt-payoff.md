Two proven strategies for paying off multiple debts systematically.

## The avalanche method

Pay off debts in order of **highest interest rate first**.

1. Make minimum payments on all debts
2. Put extra money toward the debt with the highest interest rate
3. When that debt is paid off, roll its payment into the next highest rate
4. Repeat until debt-free

**Advantage:** Minimizes total interest paid. Mathematically optimal.

**Challenge:** If your highest-rate debt is also large, progress can feel slow at first.

## The snowball method

Pay off debts in order of **smallest balance first**.

1. Make minimum payments on all debts
2. Put extra money toward the debt with the smallest balance
3. When that debt is paid off, roll its payment into the next smallest
4. Repeat until debt-free

**Advantage:** Quick wins build motivation. You eliminate individual debts faster.

**Challenge:** You may pay more total interest compared to the avalanche method.

## Which to choose

- Choose **avalanche** if you are disciplined and motivated by saving money
- Choose **snowball** if you need psychological wins to stay on track
- The difference in total interest is often smaller than expected — consistency matters more than method

## Tracking in the app

For each debt (credit card, loan), the **Analytics** section shows payoff projections. The credit card payoff chart and loan amortization chart model different extra payment scenarios, showing how additional payments affect your payoff date and total interest.

Set up **financial goals** with "debt payoff" type to track progress. Link each goal to the corresponding account and the app calculates how much you have left.

## Tips

- Always pay minimums on time to avoid late fees and credit damage
- Build a mini emergency fund ($1,000) before accelerating debt payoff
- Consider consolidating high-rate debts if you qualify for a lower rate
- Celebrate milestones — paying off an individual debt is a real achievement
