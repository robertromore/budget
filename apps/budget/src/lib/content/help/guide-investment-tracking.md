The app supports tracking investment accounts including retirement accounts (401k, IRA, Roth IRA), brokerage accounts, and HSAs.

## Setting up an investment account

Create an account with type **Investment**. In the Financial settings, configure:

- **Account subtype** — Roth IRA, 401k, Traditional IRA, brokerage, etc.
- **Annual contribution limit** — For tax-advantaged accounts (the IRS limit)
- **Expense ratio** — Your fund's annual fee percentage (e.g., 0.03 for 3 basis points)
- **Benchmark symbol** — For performance comparison (e.g., SPY)

## Contribution tracking

The app tracks your year-to-date contributions against your set annual limit. The contribution progress chart shows how close you are to the limit, helping you plan contributions throughout the year.

## Value snapshots

Since investment account values change daily with the market, the app uses **manual value snapshots** rather than individual buy/sell transactions. Record your portfolio value periodically (monthly or quarterly) in the Value Snapshots section under account settings.

With enough snapshots, the app calculates:

- **Portfolio performance** — Time-weighted cumulative return
- **Fee drag analysis** — How much your expense ratio costs over time

## Analytics

Investment accounts have specialized analytics charts:

- **Portfolio Performance** — Cumulative return over time from value snapshots
- **Fee Drag Analysis** — Actual value vs. what it would be without fees
- **Contribution Progress** — YTD contributions vs. annual limit

## Tips

- Record value snapshots on the same day each month for consistent data
- Set contribution limits for all tax-advantaged accounts to track against IRS caps
- Use financial goals to track progress toward investment milestones
- The fee drag chart helps evaluate whether to switch to lower-cost funds
