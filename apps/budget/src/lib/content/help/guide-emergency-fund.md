An emergency fund is money set aside for unexpected expenses — medical bills, car repairs, job loss, or home emergencies. It prevents you from going into debt when life happens.

## How much to save

The standard recommendation is 3-6 months of essential expenses:

- **3 months** — Dual-income household with stable employment
- **6 months** — Single income, variable income, or self-employed
- **More** — If your industry is volatile or you have dependents

Calculate your monthly essentials (rent, food, utilities, insurance, debt minimums) and multiply by your target months.

## Where to keep it

Your emergency fund should be:

- **Liquid** — Accessible within 1-2 business days
- **Separate** — In a dedicated savings account, not your checking
- **Earning interest** — A high-yield savings account is ideal

In the app, create a dedicated savings account for your emergency fund. Set a **financial goal** with your target amount to track progress.

## How to build it

### Start small

If 3-6 months feels overwhelming, start with a $1,000 mini emergency fund. This covers most common emergencies (car repair, appliance replacement, medical copay).

### Automate contributions

Create a **schedule** for a recurring transfer from checking to your emergency fund. Even $50/week adds up to $2,600/year.

### Use windfalls

Tax refunds, bonuses, and cash gifts are opportunities to accelerate your fund.

### Prioritize it

Build your mini emergency fund before aggressively paying down debt (except minimum payments). The fund prevents new debt from forming during emergencies.

## When to use it

Use your emergency fund only for genuine emergencies — unexpected, necessary, and urgent expenses. Planned expenses (vacations, gifts, car replacement) should have their own savings goals.

After using the fund, prioritize rebuilding it before resuming other financial goals.
