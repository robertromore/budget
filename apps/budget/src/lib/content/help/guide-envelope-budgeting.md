Envelope budgeting assigns every dollar of income to a specific purpose. In this app, category envelope budgets implement this method digitally.

## How it works

1. **Start with your income** — Determine your expected income for the period
2. **Assign to envelopes** — Distribute your income across category budgets (rent, groceries, entertainment, savings, etc.)
3. **Spend from envelopes** — Each transaction pulls from its category's envelope
4. **Stop when empty** — When an envelope runs out, stop spending in that category or move funds from another envelope

## Setting it up in the app

Create a **Category Envelope** budget for each spending category. Set the amount to what you intend to spend. The budget tracks spending automatically as you categorize transactions.

When an envelope runs out mid-month, you have two options:
- Stop spending in that category until next month
- Reduce another envelope to cover the overage

## Rollover

Unspent amounts roll over to the next period. This rewards underspending — if you budget $400 for groceries but only spend $350, the remaining $50 carries forward.

## Who this works for

Envelope budgeting works well if you:
- Tend to overspend in specific categories
- Want maximum control over where every dollar goes
- Have relatively predictable income
- Are comfortable with the discipline of staying within limits

## Common envelopes

| Category | Typical % of income |
|----------|-------------------|
| Housing | 25-35% |
| Food | 10-15% |
| Transportation | 10-15% |
| Savings | 10-20% |
| Utilities | 5-10% |
| Entertainment | 5-10% |
| Healthcare | 5-10% |

These are starting points — adjust based on your actual spending patterns and priorities.
