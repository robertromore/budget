The 50/30/20 rule is a straightforward budgeting framework that divides your after-tax income into three categories.

## The breakdown

### 50% — Needs

Essential expenses you cannot avoid:
- Rent or mortgage
- Utilities (electric, water, internet)
- Groceries
- Insurance premiums
- Minimum debt payments
- Transportation to work

### 30% — Wants

Discretionary spending that improves quality of life but is not essential:
- Dining out
- Entertainment and hobbies
- Streaming subscriptions
- Shopping beyond necessities
- Travel and vacations

### 20% — Savings and debt repayment

Building financial security:
- Emergency fund contributions
- Retirement account contributions
- Extra debt payments (above minimums)
- Investment contributions
- Saving for specific goals

## Setting it up

1. Calculate your monthly after-tax income
2. Create three account-monthly budgets: Needs, Wants, and Savings
3. Set the amounts to 50%, 30%, and 20% of your income
4. Assign your spending categories to each budget

## When to adjust

The 50/30/20 split is a guideline, not a rule. Adjust based on your situation:

- **High cost-of-living area** — Needs may require 60%+. Reduce wants to compensate.
- **Aggressive debt payoff** — Increase the savings/debt bucket to 30%+ temporarily.
- **High income** — You may not need 50% for needs. Allocate more to savings.
- **Starting out** — Focus on covering needs and building a small emergency fund before optimizing the split.

## Compared to envelope budgeting

The 50/30/20 rule is simpler — three buckets instead of a dozen envelopes. It works well as a starting point. Once you are comfortable tracking spending, you can transition to more granular envelope budgets for individual categories.
