Reconciliation is the process of verifying that your transaction records match your bank statement. Regular reconciliation catches errors, duplicate entries, and missed transactions.

## When to reconcile

- **Weekly** — If you enter transactions manually
- **After each import** — Verify imported transactions match your bank
- **Monthly** — At minimum, when your bank statement closes

## How to reconcile

### 1. Get your bank balance

Check your bank's website or most recent statement for the current cleared balance.

### 2. Filter to cleared transactions

On your account page, use the **Cleared** view tab to see only cleared transactions. Compare the running balance to your bank's balance.

### 3. Mark transactions as cleared

For each transaction that appears on your bank statement, change its status to **cleared**. You can do this inline by clicking the status column, or in bulk using the selection checkboxes.

### 4. Investigate discrepancies

If your cleared balance does not match the bank:
- Look for missing transactions (in bank but not in app)
- Check for duplicates (same transaction entered twice)
- Verify amounts match exactly
- Check for pending transactions that may have cleared

### 5. Mark as reconciled

Once the balances match, you can mark transactions as **reconciled** to lock them. Reconciled transactions are protected from accidental edits.

## Tips

- Use **Pending** status for transactions you have entered but that have not appeared on your bank statement yet
- Import from your bank regularly to minimize manual matching
- The running balance column helps identify where discrepancies start
- Sort by date to match transactions in the same order as your bank statement
